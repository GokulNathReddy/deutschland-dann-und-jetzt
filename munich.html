Short History

Munich was founded in the 12th century and became the residence city of the Wittelsbach dynasty. As the capital of Bavaria, it developed grand squares, royal palaces, and baroque churches. The city suffered heavy bombing in the Second World War but rebuilt its historic center. In the late 20th and early 21st centuries, Munich evolved into a center for media, engineering, and research while preserving its traditional Bavarian identity.

Culture & Lifestyle

Munich is often associated with gemütlichkeit—a cozy, friendly atmosphere. People gather in beer gardens under chestnut trees, stroll through the English Garden, and attend concerts and theater performances. The annual Oktoberfest attracts millions of visitors, but throughout the year local festivals, markets, and football matches at the Allianz Arena shape everyday cultural life.

Food Specialities

Weißwurst

A traditional Bavarian veal sausage, usually eaten before noon with sweet mustard, pretzel, and a wheat beer.

Brezel (Pretzel)

Soft, salty pretzels are an iconic Bavarian snack, a staple of Munich's famous beer gardens.

Hendl

Roast chicken often served at Oktoberfest tents, symbolizing the mix of festival culture and hearty Bavarian food.

Interesting Facts

  • Munich’s name comes from “bei den Mönchen” – “by the monks”.
  • The city hosted the 1972 Summer Olympics, leaving behind modern sports facilities and parks.
  • BMW’s headquarters and museum are located here, making Munich a symbol of German engineering.
  • The English Garden is one of the largest urban parks in the world, even featuring a surfing wave.
